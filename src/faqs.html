FAQ

Here's a list of frequently asked questions. Got one that's not on here? Send us an email

Registration

Q: Who can attend YRM2015?
A: The conference is primarily intended for PhD students in maths and related disciplines (statistics, computer science, physics). Other early-career researchers are also very welcome (e.g. masters students with some research experience, postdocs).
Q: How do I submit the title and abstract for my talk/the title of my poster?
A: After you register we will send you a link to submit the title and abstract of your talk and/or the title of your poster. Please fill this in as soon as possible and in any event by the end of Friday 3rd July.
Q: I haven’t received the link to submit my talk title and abstract.
A: Have you checked the spam folder on your email? If you still can’t find it just get in touch and we’ll send you a link.
Q: When does registration close?
A: Registration will close on Wednesday 29th July. If you want to attend the conference you are advised to register by the end of Tuesday 28th July. If you want to give a talk at the conference you must register and submit your abstract by the end of Friday 3rd July.
Q: Do I have to be based in the UK to attend the conference?
A: No, we welcome participants from around the world.
Q: Do I need a PayPal account to register?
A: No, if you are accessing the website from within the UK, you should be able to pay using a debit or credit card without a PayPal account. Just click "Buy Now with PayPal", and choose "Pay with a debit or credit card".
Q: I don't see the option "Pay with a debit or credit card", and I don't want to create a PayPal account.
A: Unfortunately PayPal can sometimes be fickle about displaying this option. Try using a different browser, clearing cookies from your browser, or using private browsing.

Schedule

Q: How does the poster competition work?
A: There will be a poster session in which delegates and judges can look at the posters, hear your explanation of your poster's content and ask you questions. There will be a prize and also glory. Full details will be confirmed nearer the time.
Q: How long are the talks?
A: Contributed talks from participants will be 20 minutes long, plus 5 minutes for questions and answers. Plenary and Keynote talks will be 1 hour long including time for questions and answers.
Q: Are refreshments provided?
A: Yes, we will provide hot lunches plus teas and coffees from Monday 17th to Wednesday 19th. There will also be wine receptions with assorted cold refreshments on the evenings of Monday 17th and Tuesday 18th.
Q: How about a conference dinner?
A: There will also be a three course conference dinner at Somerville College, Oxford on Wednesday 19th; this is limited to 180 places due to the size of the dining hall. To attend (or to join the waiting list) you just need to register for the conference and buy the 1p "conference dinner" option.

Accommodation

Q: Where is the accommodation?
A: On Turl Street, see the accommodation page. You can pick up your room key from Lincoln College porters' lodge; full details and a map will be emailed to you nearer the time.
Q: The accommodation has sold out, where should I look for alternatives?
A: The best option is University Rooms. Look out for rooms at Wycliffe, Keble, Merifield, or Magdalen as these are good value and a short walk from the conference. You might want to book sooner rather than later as the cheapest rooms are liable to sell out.
Q: Do you offer ensuite accommodation?
A: No. If you require an ensuite room you should check University Rooms or local hotels.
Q: Is breakfast provided?
A: Breakfast is not included. There are many, many cafes in central Oxford which offer reasonably priced breakfasts, e.g. the Turl St Kitchen, Brown's Cafe or Heroes. There are also branches of Tesco, Sainsbury's, Pret a Manger, McDonalds, etc.etc.

Travel and practical issues

Q: How do I access the internet?
A: Wi-fi access will be available through Eduroam. You will need to set up Eduoram with your home institution in advance. If you are staying in our conference accommodation there is a separate Wi-Fi network available there.
Q: Is parking available?
A: In general, no. If you are travelling by car, we recommend using the Pear Tree Park and Ride and catching the bus route 300, which stops opposite the Mathematical Institute. Accessible parking places at the conference venue are available by prior booking with a clear explanation of the circumstances of need.
Q: How about bike racks?
A: We have lots and lots of hoops to lock bikes to.
